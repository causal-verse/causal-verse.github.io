CausalVerse: Benchmarking Causal Representation Learning with Configurable High-Fidelity Simulations

Yunlong Deng, Peiyuan Zhu, Yan Li, Yifan Shen, Zijian Li, Guangyi Chen, Kun Zhang

Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2025

We introduce CausalVerse, the first comprehensive benchmark for Causal Representation Learning (CRL). It features configurable, high-fidelity simulations across four diverse domains, providing ground truth access to causal variables and structures. CausalVerse enables rigorous, systematic evaluation of CRL methods, bridging the gap between complex visual data and causal reasoning.

MinT: Temporally-Controlled Video Generation with Minute-Long Capabilities

Peiyuan Zhu, Yan Li, Yunlong Deng, Yifan Shen

International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2025

MinT is a novel video generation framework that accepts fine-grained temporal captions to create long, coherent videos with multiple sequential events. By conditioning on precise timestamps, our model demonstrates superior control over event ordering and timing compared to existing SOTA models, which often fail to generate events in the correct sequence.

Causal Discovery in High-Dimensional Visual Data via Latent Representations

Guangyi Chen, Zijian Li, Kun Zhang

International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2024

This work proposes a novel method for discovering causal structures directly from high-dimensional visual data, such as images and videos. By learning disentangled latent representations that correspond to underlying causal factors, our approach successfully recovers causal graphs in settings where traditional methods fail, opening new avenues for understanding complex systems.
